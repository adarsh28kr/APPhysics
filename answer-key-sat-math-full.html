Module 1 (Q1–Q22)
Algebra (Q1–Q3, Q9–Q12, Q19–Q20)
Q1 — Linear EquationsEasy (C) $10$
Solve: $5x - 8 = 3x + 12 \Rightarrow 2x = 20 \Rightarrow x = 10$
Strategy: Collect variables on one side, constants on the other. Check: $5(10)-8=42$, $3(10)+12=42$. ✓
Traps: (D) $20$ = forgot to divide $2x=20$ by $2$.
Q2 — Evaluating ExpressionsEasy (C) $24$
Substitute: $2(3)^2 - 3(-2) = 2(9) + 6 = 18 + 6 = 24$
Key: $-3(-2) = +6$. Subtracting a negative = adding.
Traps: (B) $18$ = forgot $-3(-2)=+6$, treating it as $0$.
Q3 — InequalitiesEasy (B) $s \ge 80$
"At least 80" = 80 or more = $s \ge 80$
Vocabulary: "at least" = $\ge$. "at most" = $\le$. "more than" = $>$. "fewer than" = $<$.
Q9 — Slope-Intercept FormMed (B) $\frac{3}{2}$
$3x - 2y = 10 \Rightarrow -2y = -3x + 10 \Rightarrow y = \frac{3}{2}x - 5$. Slope $= \frac{3}{2}$.
Quick method: For $Ax + By = C$, slope $= -A/B = -3/(-2) = 3/2$.
Q10 — Systems (Substitution)Med (B) $3$
$2x + (3x+2) = 17 \Rightarrow 5x = 15 \Rightarrow x = 3$
Gotcha: (D) $11$ is $y$, not $x$. Read what's being asked.
Q11 — Linear Word ProblemMed (B) $C = 40m + 75$
Rate (slope) = $40$/month. Starting cost (intercept) = $75$.
Traps: (A) reverses the fee ($75m$) and rate ($+40$).
Q12 — Interpreting SlopeMed (C) Increase per year (thousands)
Slope = rate of change = $2.5$ thousand people per year.
Key: In context, slope always means "how much $y$ changes per unit of $x$."
Q19 — Absolute ValueHard (A) $0$ solutions
$|4x-8| + 3 = 1 \Rightarrow |4x-8| = -2$. Impossible. No solutions.
Strategy: Isolate the absolute value FIRST. If it equals a negative, stop immediately — zero solutions.
Q20 — Infinite SolutionsHard (A) $1$
Simplify first equation: $x + 2y = 4$. Must equal second: $ax + 2y = 4 \Rightarrow a = 1$.
Key: Infinite solutions = same line. Reduce both to simplest form and match coefficients.
Advanced Math (Q4–Q5, Q13–Q15, Q21–Q22)
Q4 — Exponent RulesEasy (B) $4x^7$
$(2x^3)^2 = 4x^6$. Then $4x^6 \cdot x = 4x^7$.
Rules: $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$ and $x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$.
Q5 — FactoringEasy (B) $x=-3$ and $x=-4$
$(x+3)(x+4)=0 \Rightarrow x=-3$ or $x=-4$.
Sign trap: Factors have $+$ signs, roots are their negatives.
Q13 — Solving QuadraticsMed (A) $x=5$ and $x=-1$
$2x^2-8x-10=0 \Rightarrow x^2-4x-5=0 \Rightarrow (x-5)(x+1)=0$.
Simplify first! Dividing by 2 makes factoring trivial.
Q14 — Polynomial SubtractionMed (B) $2x^2-6x+6$
$(3x^2-2x+5)-(x^2+4x-1) = 2x^2-6x+6$
Key: Distribute the negative: $-(x^2+4x-1) = -x^2-4x+1$.
Q15 — Exponential GrowthMed (B) $250000(1.04)^t$
4% growth = multiply by $1.04$ each year.
Traps: (A) $0.04^t$ shrinks to 0. (D) is linear, not exponential.
Q21 — Rational ExpressionsHard (A) $\frac{x-3}{x-2}$
$\frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x+3)(x-2)} = \frac{x-3}{x-2}$
Method: Factor both, cancel common factors. Always state restrictions ($x \ne -3, 2$).
Q22 — Vertex FormHard (A) Max of $8$ at $x=3$
$g(x) = -2(x-3)^2 + 8$: vertex $(3,8)$, $a<0$ → opens down → maximum.
Key: $a<0$: max at vertex. $a>0$: min at vertex.

Module 2 (Q23–Q44)
Algebra (Q23, Q27–Q30, Q37–Q38)
Q23 — Pattern from TableEasy (A) $y=3x+2$
Slope $= (8-5)/(2-1) = 3$. At $x=1$: $3(1)+b=5 \Rightarrow b=2$.
Q27 — Perpendicular SlopesMed (C) $\frac{3}{2}$
Slope of $\ell = -2/3$. Perpendicular = negative reciprocal = $3/2$.
Rule: Perpendicular slopes multiply to $-1$: $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$.
Q28 — Systems (Elimination)Med (B) $2$
Subtract equations: $8y = 16 \Rightarrow y = 2$.
Q29 — Inequality Word ProblemMed (A) $c \le 163$
$87 + c \le 250 \Rightarrow c \le 163$.
Q30 — Function EvaluationMed (C) $9$
$f(5)=6$, $f(2)=-3$. Difference: $6-(-3)=9$.
Gotcha: Subtracting a negative: $6-(-3) = 6+3 = 9$, not $6-3=3$.
Q37 — Solving for VariableHard (A) $\frac{S-2\pi r^2}{2\pi r}$
$S - 2\pi r^2 = 2\pi rh \Rightarrow h = \frac{S-2\pi r^2}{2\pi r}$
Method: Isolate the term with $h$, then divide by its coefficient.
Q38 — Multi-Step Word ProblemHard (C) $\$860$
Monthly: $35+3(25)=\$110$. Six months: $\$660$. Activation: $4 \times 50 = \$200$. Total: $\$860$.
Break it down: Identify one-time vs. recurring costs separately.
Advanced Math (Q24, Q31–Q33, Q39–Q42)
Q24 — Simplifying RadicalsEasy (B) $5\sqrt{3}$
$\sqrt{75} = \sqrt{25 \cdot 3} = 5\sqrt{3}$.
Q31 — Completing the SquareMed (A) $(4, 4)$
$y = (x-4)^2 + 4$. Vertex: $(4,4)$.
Q32 — Exponential vs LinearMed (C) $10$ years
Test $t=10$: A $=3000$, B $=500(1.2)^{10} \approx 3096 > 3000$. At $t=9$: B $\approx 2580 < 2800$.
Key: Exponential always eventually overtakes linear. Test values systematically.
Q33 — DiscriminantMed (A) $|b|>8$
$\Delta > 0$: $b^2 - 64 > 0 \Rightarrow b^2 > 64 \Rightarrow |b| > 8$.
Q39 — Function CompositionHard (A) $3x-2$
$f(g(x)) = 2g(x)+3 = 6x-1 \Rightarrow 2g(x) = 6x-4 \Rightarrow g(x) = 3x-2$.
Gotcha: (D) $6x-4$ is $2g(x)$, not $g(x)$.
Q40 — Polynomial ZerosHard (A) $-3$
Sum of zeros $= -2$ (Vieta's). Third zero $= -2-(-1)-2 = -3$. Check: $p(-3) = -27+18+15-6 = 0$. ✓
Vieta's: For $x^3 + bx^2 + ...$, sum of zeros $= -b$.
Q41 — Rational EquationHard (A) $0$ solutions
Clear fractions: $5x + 4 = 5x \Rightarrow 4 = 0$. Contradiction. No solutions.
Key: When clearing fractions leads to a false statement, the equation has no solutions.
Q42 — Nonlinear SystemHard (C) $2$
$x^2-6x+8=0 \Rightarrow x=2,4$. $y$-values: $-1, 3$. Sum: $-1+3=2$.
Gotcha: (D) $6$ = sum of $x$-values. Question asks for $y$-coordinates.

Q43 — Circle EquationHard (B) $5$
$(x+4)^2 + (y-3)^2 = 36$. Center $(-4,3)$, $r=6$. $h+k+r = -4+3+6 = 5$.
Watch signs: $(x+4)^2$ means $h=-4$, not $+4$.
Q44 — TrigonometryHard (C) $21$ ft
Building: $40\tan55° = 57.2$ ft. Total: $40\tan63° = 78.4$ ft. Flagpole: $78.4-57.2 \approx 21$ ft.
Method: Use tangent (opp/adj) for angle of elevation problems. Subtract to find the difference.
